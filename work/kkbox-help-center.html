WEB UI DESIGN, 2015

KKBOX 
Help Center

KKBOX is Asia's leading music streaming service with over 10 million users. With the Help Center accommodating more and more contents, we soon identified the need to redesign its information architecture for better usability.

The Challenge

Users were just lost

KKBOX offers different customer service channels, including email, phone support, online customer service, and this Help Center. As service grows, it was difficult for users to find the answers they need on the content-heavy Help Center website and our customer service team had to spend lots of time answering repetitive questions. Plus, KKBOX was expanding in Japan so we also needed to configure Help Center to support multiple languages. Some major issues were:

Old navigation and categories need to be reorganized.

  • Header took up valuable screen real estate.
  • Lack of search bar, which is essential to a content-heavy website.

Poor readability and tappability.

  • The touch points for each hyperlink were small and hard to click or tap.
  • A long FAQ list, which was hard for users to skim through and find a specific topic they need.

My Role: UI Designer
Deliverables: High-Fidelity Mockups, InVision Prototypes, Zeplin Specs, and UI Kit.

Goal

To facilitate better website navigation

The overall goal of this redesign is to help users locate the answers they need more quickly, reduce customer service costs, and optimize the usability and readability of the content-heavy website.

  • Reorganize information architecture and categorize questions.
  • Provide search function to help search-dominant users to find what they want.
  • Prioritize frequently asked questions.
  • Get user feedback.
  • Use modular design, which gives website the flexibility to grow and evolve over time.
  • Complete multilingual support on the website.
Design Solution

Align with user expectations

In the redesign, the home page was divided into four sections that are most needed by the user: search, product updates, content category and song request.

We placed the search bar at the top of Help Center, inviting users to ask questions in a more direct way.

Design Solution

Optimize readability

Considering the content-heavy pages, we focused on improving readability. For example, we made the visuals as clean as possible to ensure better readability on both mobile and desktop.

Also, we added a side navigation menu so users can easily navigate on one page without going back and forth between different pages.
